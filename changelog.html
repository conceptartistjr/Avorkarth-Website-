Rulebook : Ability Scores/Combat Attributes
-- Reworked the wounds/health system for all creatures. Wounds are now split into "Wounds", "Lesser Wounds" and "Minor Wounds". These all heal at different rates, with wounds not healing unless specified.
-- Reworked non-lethal damage/wounding system to better match the new Wound system.
-- Reworked how combat damage is dealt, removing the idea of size differences and toughness, instead, damage is now simply dealt, with bonuses given for the differnce in creature's strengths. (So that a fairy punching a giant doesn't really do much.)
-- Reworked Natural Armour, it being a special talent instead of being based on a creature's constitution score.
Rulebook : Combat
-- Reworked the initiative and action system so that a creature's agility and instinct are more independant instead of being squished into a single score. Agility is how often a creature can act in a round, while instinct is now how quickly they act compared to other creatures.
-- Removed a few variation of interaction timers (Free/Immediate Actions).
-- Rewrote the rules to enacting combat to fit the new initiative and action systems.
Rulebook : Weapons/Armour
-- Reworked all of the weapons' damage and might, and changing how weapon types have changed. (This is most probably a temporary fix. It works for now, but I will be looking into these systems more in the near future.)
-- Armours are much stronger as with the removal of toughness, a creature's Constitution doesn't increase armour values at all anymore. I also changed the weapon breaking system to function better with might instead of damage. (Due to the changes in the wound systems.)

Version 0.8 - 13 August 2020

THOUGHTS/REASONINGS
-- I decided to make these changes after playing the first campaign playtest with a few friends. I noticed that the dice rolls were too high for the most part from the human races as to what I wanted the dice rolls to be. The intent behind the creation of Avorkarth was to get away from abilities and attack values of ridiculous proportions that have slowly increased in Pathfinder and DnD. The power level attained by humans in the test was a bit too high for my liking. This is especially due to the fact that I haven't yet released the upper class talents that are supposed to increase the few characters to that point of power. This update is for that reason, hoping to reduce everything and continue the idea that even getting +1 in something is a substantial power-up.
Magic is also a bit too convoluted to be easily used by players, and I have begun thinking of a better system that'll still keep the idea of magic being rare but powerful as well as separating types of casters. It'll be the focus of my next update.

CHANGES
-- Critical hits have been removed entirely, now reflected instead in the weapon damage system. This is further reflected in talents, equipment and combat rules. Likewise, critical wounds have been reworked to function as this game's version of critical hits.
-- Most magic is going to need to be re-thought and therefore the spellcasting system as it is is going to disappear. The full rework will be in a future update. (There's a lot of rebalancing needed).
-- All magic point types have been changed on how the amount of mana/energy/reverence per ability score function.
-- The Devotion/Arcana/Spirit Ability Scores have been removed from the races.
-- Caster levels/rankings have been added as a start to the change of how spellcasting works.
-- Overall damage has been redone and strength bonuses are reduced so that most average creatures are no longer one-shot, but are still in mortal danger whenever combat occurs.
-- The Ability Score system has been re-evaluated, with humans and other more common adventuring races becoming weaker overall and the allowance of negative scores in the Ability Score.
-- Added a third ability score section, replacing the magic one. Charisma and Beauty have been moved to the Social section, alongside with a new ability score called Presence.
-- Added a new natural skill called Inspire for Presence.
-- All health pools have been slightly increased, allowing for minor errors during combat. Combat is still rather quickly lethal, and most characters will still die in two to three hits in equal combat.
-- Many attributes related to Ability Scores have been rewritten due to the overall purge of power, keeping them relatively the same as to how they used to be pre-purge. (Carry weight, Languages, etc...)
-- Many class talents were redone (due to the changes and also to weaken the general, adventurer and elite talents a bit.) I have currently skipped all the magic ones.
-- Weapon damage has been redone in accordance to the Ability Score system changes, with the weapon type changing how damage functions. The split has been mostly between crushing weapons and slicing/piercing weapons' dependencies on strength.
-- Weapon/Armour materials were redone as well to compensate for the overall nerfing.
-- Updated how Skill points function, their penalties and the points allowed within, lowering the maximum bonus to dice rolls.
-- Updated the Attack/Dodge chart in combat, due to the overall Ability Score lowering.
-- Updated the additional Actions per Turn from Agility bonuses in combat, making it even harder to gain alongside the ability score lowering. Now pretty much only attainable through combat levels and talents for most common races.
-- Changed a few of the lower material values, making them more usable while still costing way less.
