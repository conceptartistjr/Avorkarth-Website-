Actions

A character's interaction with the world is show by the use of actions, or simply put, things they may do. This is split into two distinct categories. General Actions - very simple actions which the character cannot fail and which are shown in this section, and Skills which have a chance of failing, sometimes with disastrous results.

Actions

Actions are how the character interacts with the world and encompass the following :

Skill Actions: such as jumping over a cliff, acting a play or disguising oneself.

Combat Actions: swinging a sword or casting an offensive spell.

General Actions: drinking a potion, reading a scroll or speaking to another.

Each action takes a certain amount of time which is represented by the number of actions required per round.

Note! : Don't worry about writing down any general actions upon the character sheet as they are simply things that the player can tell the Game Master that they wish their character to do. Skills and combat actions, on the other hand, are used alongside dice and are therefore tracked upon the character sheet.

Action Length

Move Action : The base Move action is given freely and is not affected by swiftness. Each creature gets at least one move action per turn, in addition to their standard actions.

Standard Actions : The most common action, characters have a certain amount of actions they can do depending on their swiftness. The cost of each Action is split into Standard Actions.

Swift Actions : These actions are quick but still take up enough time that one cannot do more than a number of Swift Actions per turn equal to half their actions per turn (rounded down and to a minimum of 1).

Free Actions : These actions do not cost any time whatsoever and can be done without taking place of another action however cannot be done during another's turn.

Immediate Actions : The actions can be done during another creature's turn. Characters can only do a number of Immediate Action between their turns equal to half their Swift actions (rounded down to a minimum of 1).



Actions per Turn

Characters have so many actions per round. Actions are grouped by 2 into turns. Each additional turn is slotted at the character's initiative -4.
