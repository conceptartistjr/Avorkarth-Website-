Overview

Here you begin upon your first steps into Avorkarth. Your first task as a player will be creating a character of your own imagination (within the limits of Avorkarth) who lives and breathes within the vast realm of creation. To best describe your avatar and to remember their ability at certain tasks, it is best to fill out a character sheet. These can be found for free at Avorkarth.com. This sheet will hold all of the character's attributes, numbers and abilities for constant reference.

This primary overview will go quickly through each part of the requirements to have a readied, playable character. The details to these parts are in each of their own sections throughout the manual. The easiest way to create your first character is to grab the character sheet and fill in each required section one at a time. If you are lost or confused at the start or within any section, do not worry! Keep on reading and you'll grasp the whole complex system to creating and playing your character as the pages unfold.

Player Tip! : Before starting to create your first character, it'd be best to read through the Base sections, and to learn how the character's statistics are calculated.

BASE ESSENTIALS

Ability Scores

Ability scores are the representations of the character's physical, intellectual and instinctual abilities. They show how naturally proficient the character is at certain tasks, such as being inherently strong or having a greater ability of retaining and using information. These are separated into four categories. Action, Mind, Social and Leadership. The ability scores are the most important part of every character and greatly influence the rest of their attributes.

Attributes

Attributes, based off of the ability scores, represent the character's overall capabilities and active description. These attributes include scores such as the character's health and hit points (to see how much damage they can take before dying), their attack bonuses (to see how well they handle a sword or a bow in combat), their defenses (their ability to dodge and evade attacks), or even things such as how large they are, how far they move in a given amount of time or even amount of languages known.



CHARACTER CREATION

Race or Species

The first step of creating a character would be determining their species/race from the plethora that inhabit the realm. Any and ALL species within the world lore are playable with their own set of rules and bonuses, and a description of how they function as a species, allowing a great amount of choice for your character.

Note however, Avorkarth is primarily a human-based realm, and the humans living within tend to be rather fearful of anything unlike them and of those who do not follow their ideals and deities. Magic and magical beings have diminished over the ages with the rise of humanity and its millenia of crusades and wars, alongside forgotten histories and a silence from the deities. This has made inhuman creatures very rarely viewed near civilization, and very much hunted for their supposed tales of evils, many of which are true.

There are a few inhuman races which more commonly seen and even somewhat accepted, such as the Runnoc(dwarves), Falgrii(halflings) and Narei(low-elves).

Nevertheless, your character can be of any race or species. Just keep in mind that if you do end up choosing an inhuman race, roleplay them as they are and do your best to not humanize them, nor go outside their allowed alignments. They aren't human!

Player Tip! : The rulebook's race section holds the most common adventuring races, which are recommended for newcomers and traditional adventures. The remainder can be found in the Lorebooks.

Alignment

Alignments are the general guidelines of how your character would act upon a situation requiring their views of good, evil, law or chaos. Assigning an alignment will aid you in determining your characters actions in a pinch. Just keep in mind that there are certain races and species that have a few alignments locked to them.

Player Tip! : A good practice is usually to not choose a race or alignment that would conflict with your fellow adventurers or the Game Master's plan. Consult with the Game Master about your choice before proceeding. This usually leads to better game sessions with fewer arguments (until you argue that the GM's dice are rigged because they keep rolling perfect 10's).




Class Talents

The character's class talents sums up their vocation as well as their adventuring abilities. Avorkarth's class system isn't a singular choice and instead is a division of talents spread out over many classes from which the player can pick and choose as long as their race is able to reach this potential of power. The class talents are divided into six categories.

Player Tip! : Anything beyond the average being is rare and even acquiring a single talent grants that gap of power. Be mindful of this when creating a character and be ready to give a good backstory to the Game Master for a character of greater might.

The General talents, which are associated to no class in particular although describe abilities that rise individuals above the average of the population.

The Adventurer class talents, which are associated with individuals of incredible skill compared to the average population.
Examples : Rangers, Fighters, Rogues...

The Elite class talents, which describe the elite of each adventurer classes' fields, those who have surpassed their own limits.
Examples : Assassins, Thieves, Knights...

The Champion class talents, extolling the might of the champions of each race. These classes are often locked to certain races or species.
Examples : Wavesingers, Highlanders, Crystalguards...

The Legend class talents, which represent the rare individuals able to single handedly sway battlefields.
Examples : Sorcerers, Paladins, Grand Inquisitors...

The Mythic class talents, which are powers wielded by only a handful of individuals that roam the realm.
Examples : Moonglaives, Wuxia Grandmasters, Enchanters...

Note! : While there isn't a numerical limit to the number of talents a character can have, it can be rather frustrating for others to play alongside a character without flaws. Try your best to match the talents you choose to your character's backstory and to not overload your character with too many talents of differentiating roles!




Mastery

Levels are not a thing in Avorkarth. Instead it is based upon a mastery system. The longer a character has spent upon a skill, be it diplomacy or combat based, the better they are at such actions. If your character's backstory states that they are a veteran of battle, such as a mercenary or soldier who has trained and fought before, they'd have mastery within combat based skills. If your character is a scholar or bard, or even a thief who doesn't fight his way to the riches, then they'd probably spend their mastery upon skill sets that would benefit their backstories.

Equipment

Equipment is split into many categories. Adventuring gear, armours, weapons and so on, they are all simply the objects carried by your character to aid them on their quests and adventures throughout the realm.
Each carried equipment has a certain weight and space associated, affecting a total equipment count that the character may carry. This is dependant on the character's strength as well as their size. Keep in mind that even worn or wielded equipment still count towards the total carrying weight capacity of the character.

Magic and Spellcasting

Magic in Avorkarth is an incredibly rare thing, most don't believe it even exists. Forget about healers in every town, or potions that can cure combat wounds found around every corner. Very few beings living in Avorkarth can cast spells or create enchantments and those who can are usually hidden, since they are usually viewed with suspicion for being demonic or evil in nature due to the powers they can wield. Avorkarth is not kind to casters and many natural casting races such as the fae are hunted down or killed on sight, often mistaken for witches or daemons due to the general populace's fears.

Characters that can cast magic do so either through intense forbidden studies of the arcane laws, binding pacts with eldritch beings or with their race's innate abilities.

Spellcasting itself is based upon two aspects. The first is the spell, which is the type of magic and its intended effect. The second is power, which is the scale of the spell and how much power the caster pours into it. A shielding spell may grant a weaker caster a comforting nigh-invisible bubble that shelters them from a heated day, while a caster with much greater power can turn the same spell into a luminescant globe that allows them to shrug off a barrage of arrows or even walk through a pool of lava unharmed.
