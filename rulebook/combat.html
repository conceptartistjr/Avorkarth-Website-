Combat

From the clash of steel upon the battlefield to duels within a gladiatorial arena to a heroic warrior battling a drake upon a snowed mountain, combat within Avorkarth may happen wherever the adventurer goes. Yet all of these are replicated by following the Combat rules, no matter the encounter.

Initiating/Enacting Combat

Combat begins when the Dungeon Master declares so, usually upon the request of a player or if the players are ambushed.

A. IF AMBUSHED - (If not, go to B. - Mark Initiative)
- All characters in the combat put their initiative bonus in a list, with the ambushing creatures adding +10 to their roll.
- Any ambushed creature whose initiative is higher than any of the ambushing creatures gets to act in the ambush round as well.
- During the ambush round, creatures that can act only get a single turn, no matter their swiftness.
- The highest initiative goes first. On ties, players go first.
- Go to C. - Character Turn.

B. MARK INITIATIVE
- Characters that participated in the ambush round keep their initial initiatives, simply removing the +10 bonus.
- If any character has a high enough swiftness for multiple turns, they take their initiative roll and decrease it accordingly for subsequent turns. (See Actions - Swiftness)
- All the initiatives in the list are re-organized, with the highest initiative going first, players win ties.
- The first character takes their turn : Go to C. - Character Turn.

C. CHARACTER TURN
- The acting character gets their allocated number of standard actions on their turn and spends them however they wish.
- If an action, such as spellcasting, says that it takes one round (or more), the character must wait until their the next round before the action is capable to be enacted, even if they have additional upcoming turns. Unless specified, the character can still use the next turn's actions for movement, but must otherwise remain focused on the longer action.
-- If all characters have taken their turns : Go to D. - Round End.
-- If not : Repeat C. - Character Turn, for the next character in the initiative list.

D. ROUND END
- Once all characters have taken their turns, a round of combat is over. Most effects, such as bleeding or spell timers are calculated per round. Update all these effects.
- Calculate the Resolve for the round for each side.
- After all effects are calculated, remove any character that perished from the round from the initiative list, and have the first character in the initiative list take their turn.
- If this is after the ambush round ends : Go to B. - Mark Initiative.
- If this is after a regular round ends : Go to C. - Character Turn.



Turns vs. Rounds

Many effects happen either every so many turns or every so many rounds. These are used to distinguish between different time signatures that happen as combat occurs. Any effect that is beyon two turns only occurs once a round has passed (unless specified) (see Enacting combat).

- Turn : This is when a character does their actions, and is an approximation of a character's ability to do anything within a short amount of time.
- Round : This is only updated once every character has acted and had their initiative reset at least once. The round is an approximation of 60 seconds in real-time and is the most common time keeper for effects, with most of them occuring at the end of every round.

Attacking/Defending

Should a character wish to harm another for any reason, be it with sword or bow, they must declare an attack action. All attack bonuses are discussed in the Attributes section. This is simply the actions required to attacking. To wound another character the attacker must first hit them, then, if that is successful, must pierce their armor.

A character gets attacks equal to their Primary attacks + their Secondary attacks on their first attack action. If they use their turn to do two attack actions, then they may ONLY do their Primary attacks a second time.

To hit a target, the attacker must check their Attack Bonus relevant to the attack (Melee or Ranged) and compare it in the roll chart against the defender's Dodge attribute. If the dice's roll is equal to the required dice roll or higher, the attack hits and is carried over for the number of dice rolled for damage.

To wound a target, the attacker rolls their attack's Wound Bonus, adds the relevant other bonuses and compares it against the defender's armour attribute. Remember that the armour value sometimes changes depending on where or how the attacker is attacking. The amount of damage done to the opponent's Hit or Health points is calculated as how many points over the armour attribute the wound roll was. Wounds done by multiple hits or multiple attacks do not stack, and each instance of wounding damage to the armor or resistance attribute must be calculated seperately.



Lethal vs. Non-Lethal Intent

Sometimes, the character may wish to simply knock-out an opponent instead of killing them. If this is the case, the attacker counts any attacks with lethal weaponry as 1 roll category lower. This does not matter when attacking with non-lethal weaponry such as fists or feet. Remember that Lethal damage deals equal damage to both health and hit points, and non-lethal only to hit points.

Hitting (Attack Value/Dodge Value)

To see if an attack connects against a foe, see the Table in the Actions section.

Critical Fail (Overtaken) :
A critical failed roll has it that the defender is able to outmaneuver the attack. The attacker is struck back by their defender. Roll a die.
On a 10-8, nothing happens.
On a 7-5 the attacker is pushed back 5 feet.
On a 4-2 the attacker is also knocked prone and loses their weapon.
On a 1, the defender gets a free primary attack as if the attacker was already prone.

Fail (Dodged) :
A failed roll means that the defender was able to fully dodge the attack. The attacker doesn't hit and doesn't roll wounding damage against the defender for that attack.

Partial Success (Parried/Blocked) :
A partial success means that the defender was able to parry or block the attack. The defender adds their parry and armour values against the wounding damage.

Success (Hit) :
A success means that the attacker struck successfully. The defender only adds their armour value against the wounding damage.

Critical Success (Vital Hit) :
A critical success means the attacker was able to strike a vital weak spot of the defender. The attacker gains 2 additional damage and the defender only adds half their armour value against the wounding damage.



Striking (Might Value/Armour Value)


- Critical Fail : The weapon bounces off. Drop your weapon.
- Fail : The damage is negated.
- Partial Success : The damage is reduced by 1. (minimum 1 damage)
- Success : The damage goes through.
- Critical Success : The damage is increased by 1.

Secondary Attacks

Some characters may have alternate and additional attacks on top of their Primaries. Secondary attacks are usually those that are natural to the creature and may be added on top of any primary attacks, though only for the first attack action. As such, things like tail sweeps or bite attacks or poison glands are secondary attacks. The secondary attacks of a creature are given on their Racial page and any item with a secondary attack will be given such rule upon the item itself. Count any non-descriptive attack as one of a Primary Attack.



Special Combat Rules

Charging : A character may charge a target instead of simply moving and attacking. This uses the same amount of actions as moving and attacking, but gives the charging character +1 their Attack value. This can only be done if the character has a clear line between themselves and the target. Charges can only be done in a straight line and not over boulders or corpses.

Lesser Attack : A character may use their Move action to instead do a lesser attack. This attack is done at Attack Value -1.

Dual-Wielding : A character may wield a weapon in their off hands, up to the amount of hands the character has. For each additional weapon held, the character gains an additional Primary attack but takes a -1 penalty to their Attack value.

Flanking : If two allied characters or more attack the same target, the foe takes a -1 penalty to their Dodge value. This is increased by 1 per allied creature attacking the same target over the second. However! This only functions for creatures attacking targets of 1 size larger or smaller. This negative is added after the positioning negatives are calculated.

Difficult Terrain : Characters moving through difficult terrain are hindered and lose a certain amount of their movement speed and dodge.
Difficult terrains are split into three categories.
- Tame : -2 Movement (Minimum 1), -1 Dodge : Sand, Rocky Terrain, Undergrowth
- Harsh : Half Movement or - 4 Movement (Pick the worst, minimum 1), -3 Dodge : Brush, Marshlands, Ruin Rubble
- Severe : No Movement, -6 Dodge. : Quicksand

Attacks of Opportunity : When a foe moves by a character that hasn't used their immediate actions for the turn and the foe is within melee range, the character may do an Attack of Opportunity, doing a single Primary Attack or Combat Maneuver against the foe that ran past them.

Prone : Characters struck to the ground are immobile and are always attacked as if from the back. It takes a standard action to get back up, and offer up an Attack of Opportunity to any nearby foes.

Changing Facing Direction : The character may change their facing whenever a foe attempts to side-step them as an immediate action.

Non-lethal Strike : Characters may attack with non-lethal intent, though do so at the expense of fully commiting their strike. Any lethal weapon used with non-lethal intent reduces the attack value of the strike by 2. (see earlier)



Combat Maneuvers

Combat maneuvers are attack actions that don't deal damage, instead attempting to displace or control the target. Characters can only use a maneuver once per turn, but can use two different ones over the course of two actions. Should a combat maneuver hit, the attacker doesn't need to roll against the target's armor and the effect is automatically passed.

Size Bonus
Combat maneuvers are much harder to do to any size larger or smaller than oneself. For every size difference larger the defender gets +2 to their dodge value, and is impossible to do against any target 3 sizes larger. For every size smaller, the defender gets +1 to their dodge value.

Attack Value Penalties
Combat maneuvers require slightly more skill than simply attacking the target with a club. Difficulty checks lower the value of the attacker's attack value by the DC amount. This represents how difficult the maneuver is to pull off.

Grappling

Grappling is another Combat Maneuver, but unlike the previous maneuvers, results in a permanent state. There are two ways to initiate grappling, which is to either grab the character or tackle them.

COMBAT MANEUVERS - GRAPPLING

Combat ManeuverAttack ValueEffect
Grapple-3The character uses an action to attempt to partially grapple the target. (See Gappling).
Tackle+3The character uses an action to attempt to tackle and fully grapple the target. The target gets a free attack wound (primary only) on the grappler, ignoring their dodge.

While grappled, the target and the character both lose partial to full movement and lose bonuses to their Dodge. How much is dependant on the state of the grapple. Each round, both the grappler and the grappled target must roll and add their Strength Score x 2 to determine if the grappled target escapes.

Partial Grapple : If the grapple check is successful, both the grappler and grappled target lose half their dodge and both targets may move at half speed, although always dragging the other with them. Each target may use a single action per turn.

Full Grapple : If the grapple check is successful again or the target was tackled, the grappled target may no longer use any action that requires limbs. The grappler now also gains +5 to their strength checks to keep the grapple. If the grapple is still not broken by the grappler's next turn, the grappler may choose to either pin or choke/constrict their target.

Choke/Constrict : The grappler's checks also deal 1/2/3/4 + grappler's strength lethal damage ignoring all guarded armor bonuses.

Pin : Pinning the target ends the grapple action, and the grappler may use rope or bindings to tie up the grappled target. The target must now use their escape artist checks against the bindings intstead of strength checks against the grappler.
