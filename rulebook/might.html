Might

Legends speak of heroes of old, of champions and mighty individuals whose abilities were the greatest amongst their kind. Such power within an individual is known as might, defining those of greater ability than the average of their kind.

Class Might

Might allows to create characters of greater power and is necessary if the character wishes to gain access to the more powerful class talents. As such, might follows naerly the same progression as the class talents, and must be taken as needed. The opposite is not true, and a character may have might bonuses but not take any of the upper class talents if they so wish.

Maximum Might

While ultimately the might level of a character is chosen by the player, it is often advisable to discuss the idea with the Game Master. As with any rule that allows extremely powerful characters, might is mostly here to create more powerful obstacles. This does not mean that the player isn't allowed, nor should they shy away from Might. Simply make sure that the chosen might level matches the character's backstory and origin and is allowed by the GM.

Might Points

If a character's might is Skilled or greater, they gain Might Points, which may be spent to gain bonuses that would focus the character's speciality, alongside their chosen class talents. The maximum for each bonus category is per skill or value. (So the maximum of +2 to natural skills means as a Mythic might character, you may still spend the 10 points there, though it would need to be split between 4 points (+2) on one skill, 4 points to another (+2) and then the remaining 2 points to a final skill (+1). You may also mix and match any of the categories, as long as the spent Might points don't go over the allowed of your chosen Might.
