Combat Levels and Experience

Avorkarth is a more settled world, where the mighty and magical are rare and the skilled are just that, skilled, nothing more. Levels represent the character's ability and practice in physical combat compared to the rest of its own species. It does not represent a general power level. Weak creatures with a high level may still be weaker in combat than a mighty creature with a low level, but they will be stronger than most of their own kind.

Although levels may not give a weak character the abilities to level mountains, they still make quite an overall difference and a leveled character has a much higher chance of surviving an ambush than an unleveled one.

Maximum Level

While the experience required to gain a level is consistent for all creatures, not all creatures have the same potential of power. The highest level attainable is determined by the race and age of the character. Usually, creatures that have a longer lifespan can attain a higher level, considering they have the chance of more encounters throughout their years. This detail can be found in the race's core attriubtes table.

Experience

As your character combats various creatures, villains or monstrosities, they will get a better understanding of their physical abilities and become, through surviving it all, a better fighter. This understanding and training is represented by experience.

Characters begin at level 0! Level 1 does in fact give a minor bonus and requires experience to reach.

Experience is a number that is tracked on your character's sheet, dolled out by the Game Master for your victories in battle. Once your character has acquired enough experience to reach a level threshold (seen in the Experience to Level table), they gain the associated level up bonuses.

Experience does not reset down to 0 once a character levels up. Experience gained is cumulative and the total is what counts.

Experience Calculations

There are two main methods of gaining combat experience, combat and training, with the former being quicker but with the added unfortunate risk of dying.
- Training : Gives 1 experience per month of training.
- Combat : Gives 1 experience per every 10 successful combat actions. These are taking damage, doding an attack or attacking an enemy with a weapon while in combat. (Therefore does not include sneak attacks or similar out of combat actions.)

Average Levels

Avorkarth is indeed a land of many heroes and legends, although these are rare and far and few between. Easily 75% of Avorkarth's population is between level 0 and level 2. Even most guards tend to only reach the first or second level as anything beyond this is an impressively trained combatant. This is not to say that a character cannot be beyond this, indeed an experienced adventurer would be; It is merely a reminder that Avorkarth is a low-power fantasy land and that characters of greater power will need the proper background story as to why their power is such.

Player Tip : While your character's level is your decision, usually an adventurer with a fighting class would have had more training and combat experience than a studious sorcerer, a hermited scholar or a smooth-talking bard, therefore starting at a higher level.

Level Bonuses

Each time your character gains a level, they gain specific bonuses which get stronger the greater the level. Note as well that the level up bonuses are per multiple of the level. So at level 4, you'd gain the level 1, 2 and 4 bonuses. The Will bonuses cannot surpass the racial maximum.
