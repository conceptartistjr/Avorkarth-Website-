SYLLAN HARPY - SY'WA-EL

MEDIUM HUMANOID

Unlike the hunched and matted crow-feathered craven harpies, the syllan harpies are taller, larger clawed, more striking of pose and portrait and standing more upright, yet more ferocious and of lesser intellect. Sy'wa-el are humanoids with the torso and face of a beautiful woman and with the feathers and scaled limbs of usually a hawk, but sometimes an eagle or owl. Greater predators, they are solitary creatures that prey upon smaller creatures, lone travellers or their cousins the craven harpies.

SY'WA-EL CULTURE

Sy'wa-el live solitary lives, with a rare possibility of being a pair of egg-sisters. They nest often at the edges of forested mountainsides or in areas with high cliffs where life is abundant, avoiding desert-like wastes. Syllan harpies will also not hunt without hunger and are otherwise content to fly the skies, leaving any alone as long as their territory is not threatened. However, those below should not be complacent as their lives are safe only should they not be a sy'wa-el's next meal. The language of syllan harpies are also limited to simple sounds and calls due to their lesser intellect.

SY'WA-EL RELIGION

Syllan harpies respect great air beings such as air elemental lords, couatles and lillends and will heed their call if it is sounded. Lillends especially are regarded as keepers of a great bird-song and will often have a few syllan harpies guarding them while they fly through the nesting territories.

SY'WA-EL DIMORPHISMS

Syllan harpies are a fully female species who reproduce by egg hatching and therefore have no dimorphisms.
