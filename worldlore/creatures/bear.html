BEAR

MEDIUM BEAST

One of the most dangerous common predators found in woodlands, bears are large, muscled furred creatures, hunched on all fours. Omnivores, bears feed heavily on berries, fish and the occaisional land prey during the summer periods, preparing to fall into a deep hibernation state during the winter.

BEAR CULTURE

Bears, being beasts of lesser intelligence, have not formed a culture. However, mothers will care for their young until their growth is sufficient to live alone and unless competing for territory, bears will mostly leave each other be.

BEAR RELIGION

Bears are beasts of nature and therefore have no religion, but will respect faeic creatures as well as the greater powers and spirits of nature.

BEAR DIMORPHISMS

While the male and female of the bear species are nigh-identical in their sizes and shapes, the male bear tends to be slightly larger of frame.
