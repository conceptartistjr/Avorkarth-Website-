Skills

Skills are predetermined actions that the character may do and which require a certain amount of expertise. Unlike actions, there is a chance that the character's ability to preform these skills isn't good enough and that the character fails. The repercussions of the failure are entirely up to the Dungeon Master and the circumstances of the skill's use.

Using Skills

For a player's character to use a skill, they first declare the action they want to do. The player would then roll a die. After the required time for the skill is used, the player's skill value is then checked against the difficulty check of the action. The Dungeon Master then lets them know the outcome of the skill as well as any reactions to the skill's effect.

SKILL VALUES

Ability Score + Mastery

Any skill below 1 is counted as a skill value of 1.

Critical Fail :
A critical failed roll means disaster, often resulting in a severly negative effect.

Fail :
A failed roll means that the skill did not function, however with little to no repercussion.

Partial Success :
A partial success roll will have the skill function, but at a lesser effect than desired.

Success :
A success roll will have the effect of the skill at its normal values.

Critical Success :
A critical success roll means that the effect of the skill is greatly enhanced.

Difficulty Checks (DC)

To know if a skill has been succesful, they must check their skill value against what is known as a Difficulty Check or DC. This is a preset number given to the object or character that is the receiver of the action. The two numbers compared are used for the dice roll. (See the Dice Rolls chapter)



Natural Skills

Natural skills are ones that any character can do without learning or training. These skill aren't as heavily penalized for being untrained as learned skills, and being with a -2 penalty for being untrained.

Learned Skills

Learned skills can also be attempted by any character (as long as they aren't complex), although without learning the intricacies the character will have a much tougher task ahead. Learned Skill have a heavy penalty untrained, starting at -7, making Learned skills much harder without the relevant ability score or by being trained within them.

Complex (C) Skills

There are many skills which are marked as complex, too intricate for lesser minds. A complex skill requires that the character be of intelligence 3 or higher. This limits skills for many sections of creatures, such as animals, that cannot read nor write, at least not without magical aid.

Limited (L) Skills

Certain skills aren't able to be greatly enhanced and gain less from the character's Mastery bonuses. If so marked, they reduce any positive bonus by half, rounded down.

Knowledge Skills

While knowledge skills function just like Learned skills for their penalties, they suffer additional penalties depending on the character's backstory and upbrining due to two sections.

Knowledge - World : These knowledges are based upon reality and are divided into two major subjects: Region and Race. The character automatically begins with the Region of birth/growth and their own Race, but may acquire more with talents/backstory.
Knowledge - Magic : Knowledge Magic doesn't have any sub-sections, but instead the player may choose a sphere of magic that their character may have studied. This doesn't give them magical abilities, but can be used for knowing of magical items.

KNOWLEDGE SKILLS

Knowledge : Region (Area)

The character's total infomation known about a specified region.
Adventurer's Guess : The character may attempt Knowledge : Region checks for unknown regions, though with an additional -4 penalty.

Regional Environment : If the character's known regions share the same environment type/similar myths as an unknown region, reduce the Adventurer's Guess Penalty for Knowledge Nature/Myth checks to an additional -2 instead.

Section Knowledge - Nature
The character's total knowledge for matters of plants, beasts and the wilderness of a specified region.

Section Knowledge - Geography
The character's total knowledge about the lay of the land and landmakrs of a specified region.

Section Knowledge - Politics
The character's total knowledge about the recent historical, religious and political happenings of a specified region.

Section Knowledge - Myths
The character's total infomation known about the myths and legends of a specified region. It has been known that many regions share similar myths.

Knowledge : Race (Type)

The character's total infomation known about a specific intelligent race/civilization.
Life Experience : The character may attempt Knowledge : Race checks for unknown races, though with an additional -2 penalty if they share the same sub-type race and -4 if in good standings. This drops to -10 if the races are too far apart.
(So two human variations would have -2, any human and elf variations would have -4 and a human and a sei'yuulei nymph would have -10.)

Section Knowledge - Factions
The character's total knowledge of the many varied factions that compose the structure of a race's civilization.

Section Knowledge - History
The character's total knowledge about a specific race's ancient and recent past. Note that many secrets of many races' stories have vanished completely.

Section Knowledge - Religions
The character's total knowledge about the various religions, gods, deities and worships of a race or civilization's inhabitants.


Knowledge : Magic (Sphere)

The character's total infomation known about a sphere of magic and its associated uses, creators and benefactors.
Magically Studious : A character cannot take checks of any magic sphere without invested skill points.

THE 5 MAJOR MAGIC SPHERES

Arcana
Magic associated with matters of the arcane, from powerful law-altering rituals to the mythical leylines invisibly surrounding the world.

Elemental
Magic most commonly associated with the major elemental planes and its inhabitants, usually of fire, earth, water or air.

Light
Magic revolving around the holy worship of the Light and its defenders. Often valued through religious texts and deities such as Eonna or Arenya.

Distortion
Magic that violates the laws of creation, such as bringing back the dead with necromancy, or tearing reality itself apart to the forgotten gods' void.

Spirit
Magic from the primal, ancient force of the natural world and the very lifeforce of the realm of creation.
