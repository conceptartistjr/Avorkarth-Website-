Dice Rolls

Whenever a character, creature or player wishes to interact with the world and there is a chance that it may fail, they must roll a die. This roll is the equivalent of the character's attempt, be it good or bad.

Difficulty Check

The difficulty check for any dice roll is the opposing check, the challenge or the opponent. Whenever an action is done, it must have a counter. The Difficulty Check is often written as DC for short.

Roll Value vs. Difficulty Check

Unlike many other tabletop roleplaying games, the character's bonuses are not directly added to the value of the die. Instead, the values of both the roller and the DC are checked against one another. Depending on the difference between them, the result of the die indicates a different outcome. The greater the difference, the easier or harder it is for the roller or opponent to have a positive outcome. These are shown in the table below.




Minimum Value

Any dice roll total that is below 1 is always considered as a 1.

Example

A skillful thief is attempting to stealthily bypass a guard to get to the treasure. Being skilled at thieving, they have a skill value of 8 for their move silently skill.
The guard would counter this with a listen check when the thief gets in range (or every time the thief decides to stop then move again). The guard is an average human with a listen skill value of 6.
This would mean that the listen check is less than the Difficulty Check (the thief's move silently). The guard would therefore roll a die. On a 1 to a 3 he would critically fail, on a 4 to a 6 he would fail, on a 7 to an 8 he would have a partial success and on a 9 or higher, he would hear the thief fully.
