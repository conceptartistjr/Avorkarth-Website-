Ability Scores

Abilities are the core representation of every creature's general aptitudes, each noted by a flat number, a tier, which in turn gives a certain bonus to attributes, skills and dice rolls. These determine the creature's strength, swiftness, bravery, agility and so on are used as the basis for most other rules and actions that interact with the world.

Ability Categories

Abilities are split into distinct categories. These are the Action, Mind, Social and Leadership abilities, each governing a part of the character's whole.

Racial Abilities

Every race within the beastiary is assigned an ability tier and score in all four categories. The number shown represents the normal, average habitant or traveller. However, each individual, even among the masses, can be gifted, trained or simply better or worse at certain of their aspects. This is represented by increasing and decreasing ability tiers.

Greater or Lesser Abilities

When creating a character, the player may choose to create a character of greater or lesser power than the average if they so desire. Keep in mind that such characters over the average are rare, and any increase in base ability scores beyond the average stated upon the race's page is an incredible achievement. There are very few that have within them the drive to push themselves to this potential.

Method 1 : Choice : At character creation, you may choose to increase or decrease any ability score that would match the character's backstory. Any increase cannot be higher than the racial ability score maximum.

Method 2 : Dice Roll : Roll a die for each ability. Although each race has a different maximum ability score, you may choose to roll as follows, or any other approximate roll that would function.

ABILITY SCORE ROLLS

RollEffect
1Lower the ability tier drastically.
2-3Lower the ability tier by 1.
4-8Don't change the ability tier.
9Increase the ability tier by 1.
10Increase the ability tier drastically.

Remember that you cannot go beyond the maximum of the racial ability score!

Approximate Ability Scores

For players who have a bit of trouble knowing how many ability score additions are appropriate, it is best to consult the Game Master for their campaign. Though, if still unsure, below are some approximations for a karthian human variant character (considering that even +1 to an ability score is an incredible increase).

Average : -3 to 0 Additional Ability Score.
Skilled : 1 to 2 Additional Ability Score.
Talented : 3 to 4 Additional Ability Score.
Blessed : 5 to 7 Additional Ability Score.
Legendary : 8+ Additional Ability Score.




Player Tip! : Ultimately, it is best to try and match the backstory and direction of the character. Should you wish to add points into Intelligence and Wisdom but remove points from some physical scores to create a weak but knowledgeable scholar, there is no reason not to. Same, it is entirely possible to play as a simple villager adventuring for their first time by not increasing any ability and choosing no class talents. As with all things, keep in mind the other players and their playstyles as well.

ABILITY SCORES

Action Abilities

Strength (Str)

Strength represents a character's muscle quantity and raw physical power. Characters with high strength can carry more objects as well as strike harder. Strength increases the chance of breaking through armor with physical attacks, increases the character's carrying capacity (though not space) as well as increasing the effectiveness of skills that have to do with muscled exertations such as climbing or swimming.

Agility (Agi)

Agility represents a character's nimbleness and movement. Characters with high agility can move faster and with more ease than most while also having an easier time crossing over acrobatic obstacles. Agility increases movement speed, actions per turn, ease to hit, turn initiative and skills that have acrobatic components.

Dexterity (Dex)

Dexterity represents a character's attention to small movements and finesse. Characters with high dexterity have an easier time with detailed movements such as picking locks or pockets or crafting a detailed object. Dexterity is used to increase many skills that provides characters alternatives instead of face to face combat, although also aids in striking opponents with ranged weaponry or dextrous fighting styles.

Constitution (Con)

Constitution represents a character's vitality and health. Characters with high constitution tend to outlive dangerous situations while also overcoming poisons and diseases more easily. Constitution is used in conjunction with the Race's base health attribute to determine the character's maximum hit/health points, their resistance to toxins and poisons and many special physical actions such as keeping one's breath.



Instinct (Ins)

Instinct represents a character's overall ability to sense dangers and react to them. Characters with high instinct tend to find themselves in fewer dangerous situations than others, reacting to situations almost before they happen. Instinct directly impacts a creature's initiative in combat, the ability to intuitively dodge attacks and any skill which requires sensing their opponent.

Mind Abilities

Intelligence (Int)

Intelligence represents a character's book knowledge and memory retention. Characters with high intelligence are usually well read and well versed in the mysteries of the world. Intelligence is most notably used for scholar characters as it determines the number of languages known and their well of knowledge upon chosen subjects.

Wisdom (Wis)

Wisdom represents a character's action knowledge and ability to read people. Characters with high wisdom will usually know what to do and how to react in dangerous situations. Wisdom therefore increases many skills in which being mindful is the most appropriate action.

Social Ability Scores

Charisma (Cha)

Charisma represents a character's overall ability to persuade and influence. Characters with high charisma seem to know exactly which words will make things go their way. Charisma is very useful for characters who wish to resolve situations through speech and reasoning.

Beauty (Bea)

Beauty represents a character's overall physical attractiveness and elegance. Characters with high beauty have an easier time being accepted by most races and will find themselves in less trouble overall. However, beauty without wit or charm only gets a character so far and is the least effective method of influencing a target.



Leadership Abilities

Presence (Pre)

Presence determines the how dignified, imposing or majestic a character is, how well they command the room and are able to bring attention to the preferred subject. Characters with high presence are inspiring, able to better encourage their allies or terrify their opponents.

Will (Wil)

Will represents a character's overall ability to resist outer influences. Characters with high will are not as easily persuaded nor scared and are able to better resist magical domination effects. Unlike all other ability scores, Will is never used for rolls and is always simply the number to beat. Therefore, the racial base Will is usually higher than other ability scores.
