Spellcasting

Upon the rarest of nights there are some that swear of hearing incantations upon faintest of winds, and feel that the world has somehow changed. This belief of magical and supernatural superstitions is often laughed at, mocked and belittled, though could not be more true. Magic, though extremely rare, is very much a part of Avorkarth. Eldritch powers and ancient spiritual beings are present within the deep areas of the world, while servants to the gods enact rituals to gain favor and blessings for their kin or personal power and wizards construct arcane studies that live long after they pass from the age as newly attuned are born in the cycle.

Spellcasting Rarity

Magic is truly rare within Avorkarth, and most of its mortal races live their entire lives without its presence, especially its most common, humanity. The magical and supernatural are hidden away and even beneficial magic is looked upon with fear and distrust. Only answered prayers that come from some divine calling or a miracle from the light based gods are at times viewed with grace and kindness among the commonfolk, and even then, usually only if it is from the gods of their race. Should a non-magical character wish to become a spellcaster class, or has magical powers due to racial benefits, they would best be weary of using such things around the commonfolk, for not all react well to it.

Although magic is extremely rare, it is equally powerful, the lowliest of spells easily being equal to the might of a sword and able to turn the tide of a battle.

Spellcasting Types

Magic at its core is split into three types :
- Devotion, which is magic gifted from divine powers or ancient beings used by the priests, worshippers and enforcers of certain deities or mighty beings.
- Arcane, which is magic that is derived from the laws of the world, usually used by wizards, necromancers or sorcerers and other traditional casters.
- Spiritual, which is the root of natural life that flows throughout the world, often used by fae-folk, eldritch creatures or similar natural casters.

While casting spells is split into three distinct styles, the spheres of magics and their sub-sections, such as Elemental, Necromantic and Demonic are the actual realms from which casters draws their powers. Elemental magic, as an example, is shared between Arcane and Spiritual, the former controlling it and the latter borrowing it, but both Arcane and Spirit casters are able to cast from its Sphere with varying effects.



Divine/Devotion - Prayers and Litanies

Though still rare, devotion spells are the most commonly seen. Devotion spells are viewed as miracles or divine intervention and rightly so. Divine spells are incantations, prayers and callings upon the worshipper's deity. Through the proper words, the belief of the caster, their fervor to the deity being called and the intervention of the deity itself, devotion spells are able to be cast.

Given Power : Characters have access to all the spells given by their deity, as long as they have the mana to spend. These can be called upon and cast at any time unless specified. Each deity has a list of spells under their names in the Lore section along with their effects.
Deity's Chosen : Devotion casters also gain bonuses given by the deity. These are listed right before the spell list.
Place of Worship : If a character is within a sanctified area of their deity, they increase their caster might by 1 for spell costs and effects.



Arcane/Ley-Line - Incantations and Spells

The mystical art of sorcery is one shrouded by mystery and superstition, yet is one of powers beyond imagining. Arcane spells are similarily cast with incantations, yet only function as an infusion of the caster's magical attunement alongside their knowledge of the spell itself. To gain attunement to the arcane, practitioners will usually study ancient texts and tomes, have a well of power already within them, or find a darker path of bargains with ancient powers.

Spell Memorization : Characters who study the arcane can memorize a certain number of spells equal to their twice their intelligence score.
Arcane Tome : Arcane casters must write down all of their magical incantations and spells that they've discovered. This tome allows arcanists to spend a single action to find any non-memorized spell that they've learnt or discovered, which then may be cast during this or the next round of combat.
Changing Memorized Spells : Arcane casters may rememorize different spells by spending a 8 hours reading their Arcane Tome. These must be spells written in their tome and they may memorize a number of spells equal to their intelligence score.



Spirit/Natural - Unbound Power

The elusive and nearly invisble power that creates life itself is known to very few and detectable by ever fewer. This power that has been within all things since the beginning of the Realm of Creation is most present and powerful within lands of unbound growth. It is the basis of all ancient and spiritual magics, echoes of the first ages' songs. Spiritual magic appears within areas of the world where nature is respected and a more primal view is upheld. For this reason, it has been disappearing, its subtle flow ignored by the fast paced lives of most younger mortal races and dissipating from areas of populated civilization and reckless construction.

Inner Power : Characters attuned to Spiritual magic may cast any spell from within their sphere of magic without restriction. Spiritual casting is similar to a whim or a feeling more than a precise incantation. The might and matter of the spell depends more on the power within the caster than any other reason.

Mana

All spellcasters use an invisible force that is throughout the world. While each caster type also uses it in a slightly different manner, it essentially has the same origination. Any caster character of note has 5 mana base, increasing by 1 per caster might category. (see below). The amount of mana used per spell is based upon the power of the spell and the amount of time used to cast it.

Regaining Mana

Each caster type regains 1 mana per day, with special actions to speed up this process.

DEVOTION CASTERS
Prayer : The character may sit in uninterrupted prayer to their deity, regaining 1 mana point per 12 hours. If they have an icon of worship with them, increase the bonus by another 1. If the character is within a sanctified area of their deity, they reduce the time to gain their reverence by half, regaining their mana every 6 hours instead.

ARCANE CASTERS
Ley Reserve : Arcane casters naturally regain an additional mana per day.
Syphon : The caster may touch any arcane magical object to regain mana equal to the level of the spell on the item. The magical item loses all magical qualities until energy is refocused into the item. Should the level of the spell on the magical item surpass the caster's might, they take 3 lethal damage per spell level higher.




SPIRIT CASTERS
Nature Bound : Spirit casters regain 1 mana per X amount of time, slowing significantly while in areas without spiritual life.

Spellcaster Might

Spellcasters are not all equal in power. Such rankings can be categorized, with certain thresholds of power granting the spellcaster additional magical might. These are the spellcaster ranks, which are dictated by the the character's race or class talents. The greater the caster's category, the more spells they can cast and the greater their power.

Non-Casters : For all rules and specific situations, non-casters are considered as having a spellcasting might of -3 and a "mana" pool equivalent of 2.

Spell levels and Cost : Each spell level doubles the cost of the previous level. This is intended to make every caster level truly matter.

Casting Spells

Whenever a spellcaster wishes to use magic in any form, they must weave the mana flowing through the world into a tangible result. This is the art of spellcasting. While dependant per the power and ability of the caster, magic is technically cast in the same way for all. It requires the spell, the caster's own power spent and an amount of time.

Casting Time

To all spellcasters, time is a very important matter as the greater the caster's concentration upon a spell, the less it costs upon their body and mind. Casting time is based upon how many actions it takes, show in the table below.

Time Limitation : Spells do not benefit from Swiftness, and only two standard action spells may be cast per round. Should two standard action spells be used, only movement actions may be used for the remainder of that character's round.

Cantrips/Orisons : If a spell cost is lower than 0.25, it is considered free. A caster may cast up to their caster's might of free spells per turn.

Ritual : If a spell cast time is greater than an hour, it is considered a ritual. (See the ritual section).

Spell Levels

Depending on how much mana is spent into spells, they may grow exponentially in power. Whenever a certain amount of mana is spent, the spell is upgraded to the next tier and all the effects increase as well. Whenever a spell is upgraded to its next level, the caster may choose to use any component of it from a lower level.

Certain spells have higher base levels, meaning that the effect of the spell is too high at its very base, although they may also be upgraded. Similarily, some spells cannot be increased any further than a certain level. All of this is shown per spell.




Permanent Spells

Most spells can be made permanent withou the caster requiring to keep concentrating, however the cost to do so is tremendous. Any spell, enchantments or similar effect that is to be permanent (such as creating a magic weapon), increases the relative cost at -16 caster might. Most often it takes a ritual to do any such effect.

Magic Spheres

All magic is contained within their particular field, also known as a magic sphere. Certain magical spheres have sub-realms of casting within themselves. The magic that the caster is capable of casting is given by the class talents and racial bases, which also defines which of the major magic types it is. Even devotion magic casting is simply a method of accessing a sphere of magic, held in partial control by the deity being called upon.

Counter-Spelling

Whenever a spell is first cast, the surge of energy that occurs is naturally felt by any others nearby. Should another spellcaster be near the caster of the spell, they may attempt to dispel it as an immediate action by spending part of their mana to disrupt the energy flow. They may drain their own mana until the point it equals the level of the spell being cast. For each point over their maximum mana, the counter-speller takes 1 point of lethal damage up to half their maximum health points, at which point the counter-spelling fails. The spent mana used does not return. If the opposing caster is not the same caster type, increase the caster might cost by 1.

Rituals

Not all caster characters are mighty enough to cast certain spells, nor are most able to cast any whatsoever. However, with the aid of the right words, movements and ingrediants, characters may empower their capabilities beyond what is normally available to them. Rituals can also be empowered by multiple people, gaining ever greater might, which have in the past enabled cults to raze the cities of their enemies with magical plagues or to awaken and control eldritch creatures deep within the earth.
Rituals require the proper ingrediants and symbols, and much like arcane spells, must be known by the initiator. If any part of the ritual is disrupted or moved, the spell breaks down. Spells that are quick cast, such as fireballs or rays, often have the ritual make the user inscribe the magic into an item like a wand or staff. Each spell has its required components written.

Casting a Ritual : Once all is set-up, then the ritual may be cast. Rituals reduce the total of the spell's cost by 1 per hour spent on the spell, with each point reduction costing one additional hour.

Example : A non-caster wishes to create a ritual for a 1st level spell. Considering a non-caster has a mana pool of 2, they would require to bring the spell cost to below that. So by spending at least an hour on the spell they may consider their caster might as 2 higher, equivalent to a 0th might caster. This would mean that a 1st level spell now costs 6. To reduce it to 2, they must reduce it by 4, so it would take 10 (1+2+3+4) days to complete the spell.

Casters and Rituals : Casters of higher level than the original ritualist may use their own magic to speed up the ritual. If they remain focused for at least an hour, they reduce the original time to their own caster might +3. Any already elapsed hours then further reduce the cost by 1 per.

Multiple Ritualists : For every additional participant in a ritual, reduce the time required by 4 hours.

A 6 day ritual with two participants would reduce it to 5 days and 8 hours while with three participants it would be reduced to 5 days and 4 hours.

Feedback : Any ritual or spell cost that is greater than 32 FROM THE ORIGINAL CASTER MIGHT has an ever increasing chance of killing the caster. Every hour, roll a die per mana higher than the cost of 32. Each die that rolls a 4+ makes the caster take 1 Lethal point of damage. Should the original caster die due to the Feedback, the next main "caster" is chosen randomly or by any who step up. The ritual now checks if the ritual is higher than the cost of 32 for the new main caster and the process repeats.



Spells

This section is just an example of how to read spells and gives very basic spells for those who chose to play casters with only what's available within the rulebook. The actual spellcasting, deity, spells lists are in the lore section. Most casters are much too rare for base adventurers, but players who wish to do so may with the additional information in the lore books.

Spells have multiple components, each with their specific information.
Spell : The name of the spell. While Devotion spells always have a singular name, Arcane and Spirit spells names change depending on the amount of power poured into the spell.
Level : The level and approximate power of the spell, and therefore cost.
Sphere(s) : The overlapping spheres of magic that the spell belongs to.
Range : How far away the spell can "land" or be cast away from the caster.
Reach : How large of an impact area the spell has from its range base point. The caster may choose to limit the reach of a spell to a minimum of a single 5ft. square, but cannot make it larger than the Reach value.
Save : The dice roll, attribute or ability score required to resist the effect of the spell. Most commonly used with mind control or will based spells.
Cast : The type of spellcast the spell is, these are :
-- Once : The spell is cast once, lasts a certain duration, then ends.
-- Continuous(Cont.) : The spell is cast then maintainted. While the spell is maintained, the caster cannot regain the mana spent on the spell until they let go of the spell.
NOTE! : If the caster takes their time with a continuous spell to reduce the cost, the drain is still equivalent to the ORIGINAL COST of the spell! Mana CAN go into the negatives due to this and will take more time to recover!
