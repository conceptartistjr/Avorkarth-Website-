Race

The first step of any player going through character creation would be choosing their race from among the many that live and breathe within Avorkarth. While this chapter doesn't list all of the available races, since the player can choose pretty much any within the lore section, it provides the details and know-hows of how to read and use any race's information. Below are also shortened versions of the most commonly seen adventuring races in the midlands of Avorkarth, enough to get any player their first character.

Racial Description

Every race or species within the lore book will have a description of their culture, appearance and lore. This serves to aid the player in understanding what they are roleplaying as, and offers nice minor details which they can play off of. The descriptions in this rulebook are shortened, so that first time players aren't bogged down with details and can focus on learning the basics.

Racial Core Attributes

The Core Attribute Scores represent the creature's base average characteristics, the basis of the race's field potential and ability within situations of danger. These are numbers such as their movement speed, natural armor and their base vitality against wounds.

Racial Combat Skills

Every creature fights for their life in one manner or another. Humans, with no claws or teeth to speak of, have turned to crafting and smithing to create truly deadly weaponry. The combat skills demonstrate the species' aptitude while using any natural or wielded weapon.

Creatures without crafted weaponry rely upon their natural evolution, often in the form of teeth or claws. These are natural attacks, and are always available to the creature should they not be currently wielding weaponry.

Racial Ability Scores

Ability Scores are simply numbers representing any creature or character's aptitudes. They are the baseline scores that almost all other numbers and dice rolls are based off of, and show the very basis of what the character is capable of.
The •'s below the numbers are how many increases in which a character of the chosen race can increase their score. Keep in mind that anything beyond the average starting number is rare, and the peak potential cannot be bypassed without magical aid!



Racial Dimorphism

Some races have a distinct difference between the male and female of their species. Although for humans and many other of the more common races this difference is minor, there are some in which the species will get many additional statistics or abilities depending on their gender.

Racial Alignments

While a race often acts the same way to a certain extent, it is composed of individuals, each with their own thoughts and habits. Those variations of thoughts are the alignments, dictations of how a character responds and acts to certain scenarios. Note that not all races can be of all alignments, as that curse or blessing is mainly reserved for humanity.

Racial Talents

Each race within the beastiary has racial talents, similar to class talents. These are bonuses that are added upon the base statistics. Unlike class talents however, racial talents must be taken and cannot be picked or chosen. If you truly do not wish to take one of the racial talents due to conflicting backstory, talk of it to the Game Master and see if they are all right with this choice.

Racial Cultures and Major Faiths

The faiths and cultures of a culture define how most creatures of that species act and respond to the world on a day to day basis or in times of dire consequences. While each creature has major faiths associated alongside their race, there are always individuals who discover or worship other beings, thus allowing the player to choose another religion or even create one if they wish. Understand that these personal worships aren't always supported by Game Masters, nor will they be lore specific. Additional faiths and deities can be found in the lorebook.



Humanity

Though humans are not the eldest, nor the strongest, nor the most agile of races, they are the most common upon the continents. This is due to the fact that humans are flexibly adaptive to most environments and opportunities, harshly judge those that are not alike and spread really, really quickly. Add to that a suprisingly high potential for great minds, and humans have risen to create a dominant civilization upon the central lands of the realm.

Humanity has survived many ages but their history has often been rewritten, destroyed or simply forgotten due to their warlike tendencies. Since their age dawned many millenia ago, humanity spread and split into many variants, each evolving depending on their settled area. These are as follows :

KARTHIAN CULTURE

Though Karthians are united by their creed, at heart theirs is a turbulent culture. The kingly thrones that govern the six kingdoms are highly sought and the line of kings changes hands semi-regularly. Wars between the six kingdoms, or even within, have quickly broken out if no other threat is brought to attention. As such, Karthians are proud of their humanity first, their kingdom second and their servitude last, but will unite, lock arms and stand together without hesitation against any outside threat. This unfortunately also leads to most Karthians being immediately suspicious of any who are not karthian, especially if not human and truly not if inhumanesque.

SHARASSIAN CULTURE

The Sharassian civilization is known to have been raised upon puzzles and secrets, and a Sharassian's love for a riddle or is said to be equal to a sphinx. A Sharassian will be cautious and questioning, always seeking to know the entirety of the problem before striking a deal. For the most part, they are a trade-based people with few major cities and lots of travelling camps that dot the desert dunes. Like Karthians, Sharassians are a united people should a threat arrive, but otherwise, the lure of controlling a life giving oasis or a rocky shaded crag that shelters from the environment has often touched upon base human greed and want.


SHARASSIAN RELIGIONS

The harsh deserts often lead to Sharassians asking their many Gods for help. Each Divine has an aspect that they control and oversee, though are all under the laws of Orsir, Lord of Sands, savior of the Sharassian culture when their ancient cities were swept under the sands. As such, the worship of another deity isn't as strange to Sharassians as they believe that there is perhaps another facet of a phenomenon which is controlled by an unknown minor God.
There is another prevailing goddess in Sharas, Skressis, she whom is presumed dormant. Deep in the Vildaar desert lies the bones of a massive serpent which tower over the dunes. The bones are impossible to chip with any known tools of iron or steel and as such has gathered worship over the centuries, the priests chanting that only the faithful will live when Skressis awakens to end this age.

TILDRAN CULTURE

The Tildran have long centered their culture around covens of what most would call witchcraft, with each tribe having a matron council that advises a single chosen male warlord. Bones, blood and flesh have great meaning to them and it is not uncommon for a Tildran to keep a dried bone of previous family members on them at all times. However, after their great loss against the Karthan empire, many have now begun instead attempting to emulate their Sanguinnar saviors. With this turn, many tribes have cast out the olden ways and are forsaking the eldritch spirits of their ancestors, awaiting their turn to be one of the chosen that the Sanguinnar priests bring back with them to their golden lands.


TILDRAN RELIGIONS

Religion is split within the Tildran people. The first is their coven worship of the eldritch beings, those that bestow their great powers to the few witches that guide the tribe. The second, which is stronger the closer the tribe is to the edges of Tildran land, is the similar worship of the Karthians, being the Holy Light of Eonna and her three archangels. The third, and newly rising in the north-west tribes is the worship of the Sanguinnar's Eternal King, for the myths in his supposed achievements name him to be a newly arisen God.

THRAKAEN CULTURE

Thrakaen society is based upon honour and combat, where the highest honour one can have is to die defending a friend or a hearth. Warring clans and villages would often bury and chant the fallen dead of both allies and foes at the end of conflict for their bravery upon the bloody snow. The Thrakaen clans are lead by an elected chieftain, often the wisest or the strongest amongst them.
Though once the clans warred and battled amongst themselves, the Karthian invasion half a decade ago united many of them and they were successful at pushing the legions back to a standstill. At the war's end, Thrakaen's culture was split into two, as were their lands. Most clans have chosen peace and more and more follow the ways of Karthia, while a few remaining northern clans hold to the wilds and fight still , ready to reclaim their ancestral homes when the time returns.


THRAKAEN RELIGIONS

Thrakaen respect deeds of valor above all things, and their beliefs reflect this. They worship the fallen dead with chants of their stories and heroic deeds, calling to their pantheon of gods to let the brave feast within the halls of Oderyn. Thrakaen also respect and pray to various spirits of nature, their myths filled tales of such creatures, but they don't consider them gods, merely as creatures that carry out the will of the pantheon above.
The rise of Eonna's worship has also emerged from the southern most tribes who trade with the new Karthian footholds. This is met with more resistance the further north one goes, where the old traditions still hold sway.

XIA CULTURE

Xia's culture is very much an odd one to most other human variations as it hinges mostly on philosophy and wonder. Questions asked can go through debates for centuries before simply being left alone, becoming another of the thousand mysteries of Jian. Their reverence of respect is also obvious as even the most hated of Xia brethren will often speak to one another before they act upon the more primal instincts of warfare. This makes it very strange for the more straightforward races to live alongside the philosophical Xia, their direct questions toyed with and tossed around only for a hundred different answers to fall from the heavens above, each a plausible solution.


XIA RELIGIONS

The Xia religion is the worship of the spirits of the land. These can manifest in just about anything, and almost everything has a spirit associated with it. Xia therefore mostly pray and give offerings to the spirit that watches over their land, known as a Land God. Spirits however, are not all benevolant. The Xia therefore seprerate spirits into two categories, the Ki'tzai (Kind Spirit) that watch over humanity and nature, and the Ki'tzau (Evil Spirit), known to eat human souls and care only for themselves. Yet above all Land Gods, spirits and men, there are supposedly five great Spirit Gods, watching the very balance of Jian itself and each taught their ways by the august First Heavenly Emperor, breather of life.

DAELDEN CULTURE

Daelden culture has not changed in centuries, still holding on to the elder ways of a singular king that takes care of all people. His court is represented by a line of families ranging back to the earliest recorded histories, though with new families being newly appointed should a line end. The Daelden have very little poverty or trouble, being that they are fewer than other human variations and mostly kind to one another. Their cities lie in ruins, overgrown and taken over for but the few areas still being lived in. This makes many Daelden roamers and rangers, wandering through the other human lands and lending aid and aged wisdom where they can.


DAELDEN RELIGION

The worship of the light of Arenya reings supreme within the Daelden culture. Representations of her splendor, as well as the many angelic statues of her servants line the old cities, carving a beauty of olden times and of a greater humanity who adored a goddess of life and light. Ul'gath, he who guides the dead to their end, is also given place, often in graveyards and massive mausoleums. The Daelden do not seem to easily deviate from this worship, especially if they grew within the heart of their people, and as such, even though worshipping another deity is not frowned upon, it is rather rare.

FALGRII CULTURE

Although most races would get irritated by the constant forgetfulness of their pride, the Falgrii welcome it with laughter and a wide smile. Falgrii culture is one of peace and tranquility, their homes far from most horrors of war and rather left alone by their Karthian, Narei and Runnoc neighbors. Fishing, farming and gardening are highly looked upon by the community and baking and brewing is viewed as the greatest of professions. Falgrii are often weary of larger folk but will nonetheless throw a feast and party, often under the misapprehension that such a feast wouldn't take place even without the visit.


FALGRII RELIGIONS

The Falgrii are rather relaxed when it comes to religions, as they are with most matters of life. Their close proximity to their Karthian, Runnoc and Narei neighbors has often given them a plethora of religions to follow, but the central human ones are the most common amongst the outsider views. Yet the most prevailant, and unique to their own kin, is the worship of the one known as Old Leafbeard, also known as the Mossman or the Greenman. Stories say that it was this great being that formed the rolling hills, winding creeks and green pastures that the Falgrii do so enjoy, and that he keeps ever watch on them.

RUNNOC CULTURE

Runnoc law is absolute. There is no deviation, there is no faltering, the roots of the mountains is where the written word is unwavering. Runnoc culture is centered around their language, which almost only deals with absolutes much like mathematical equations. As such, their civilizations are incredibly stubborn, but very fair. Should the word of the law favor one, it will do so without wavering, only ever changing should another truth be unveiled. However, while their laws and rules may seem slightly extreme to any outsider, it does not mean that they do not enjoy life. Their liking of drinks and foods rivals that of the Falgrii and their deep stone halls bear witness to bountiful feasts by roaring fires.


RUNNOC RELIGIONS

While the statues lining the halls of the Runnoc are given to their Kings of old and the memories of the greatest smiths and rulers is prevalent in the Runnoc culture, their worship is given to a singular God, no matter the mountain hall. Volkayn, the greatest smith, the father of all dwarven kin, he who shaped the primal world into the mountains and valleys which house the Runnoc. Entire sub-sections of the mountain caves, often those with the most precious metals and gems, is given to monumental representations of Volkayn and his immortal sons. However, ever since their trade with their above-ground neighbours, the most common religion of Eonna has found its way into the minds of a few Runnoc.

Ælvenkin

The eldest remaining civilization of the second age of the Realm of Creation, the aelvenkin, or elves as named by humanity, had discovered sun based arcanic manipulation and harnessed its secrets to create their rule. This age ended after thousands of years of opulence when they failed to stop their own insane lust for power and the unfortunate mastery of blood magic. The revolt of the lower class along with the greatest invasion of the southern dark orcs tribes of the age split their species thin and led to the destruction of most of their ancient civilization.

This split their race into three. The now more common Narei, remnants of the lower class who left the old ways to learn to live anew, the remaining Sanguinnar, who closed the golden gates to what is left of the empire of the crystal sun, and the very few Sildaen, who followed the greatest mind of their race, a prophet who had predicted the fall long before it happened. (The Sildaen use the Sanguinnar statistics and rules, except for the blood ritual.)

While some have speculated that aelvenkind and humanity are actually of the same descent due to their remarkable similarity, with only the slightly elongated ears and sharper eyes marking a difference, most agree that this is not the case and many Sanguinnars are outright offended by such claims.



AELVENKIN RACIAL TALENTS

While both variants of aelvenkin split millenia ago, they still do share many common traits. These racial talents must be taken no matter which variation the characters ends up being.

- Keen Senses : Gain +1 to the Listen, Search and Spot skills.

- Arcane Ancestry: Gain +1 Arcane resistance.

- Lithe Movements : You may reroll any 1's for the Jump, Climb, Balance and Move Silently skills.

- Low-Light Vision : Gain the ability to better see in darkened areas. Your vision is counted as low-light.

NAREI CULTURE

Narei have been changing and evolving their culture ever since they left their ancestral golden lands. Wandering from land to land, their kind finally settled upon uninhabited forests near both the Karthian and the Falgrii. Using all adopted cultures they had met in their travels, the Narei created a small hidden kingdom of their own to shelter their kind. As such, the Narei culture very much resembles that of the adaptive Karthians, though with less account for personal weatlth and power. Ruled by a line of kings, they are nonetheless advised by an elected council, keeping to the ideal that every voice should be heard.


NAREI RELIGIONS

The religious beliefs of the Narei is founded upon the olden ways of their ancestors. It was said that long before the crystalline discoveries, the Aelvenkin worshipped Arenya much like humanity. Many Aelvenkin, including Narei, still have a hard time believing this as the tales of Ashna, the Eternal King are still revered and they hope that he shall reawaken to reunite all Aelvenkin back to their glory of old.

SANGUINNAR CULTURE

The last remaining cities of the Sanguinnar are still those of opulence and decadence. They mostly ignore the outside world, their high priests clouding their minds with the lies that all is still well, and that their immortality is given freely. Most Sanguinnars know where the flow of endless blood comes from, but their culture views all others as necessary sacrifices and is reflected in their arrogant demeanors. While a few Sanguinnars have since come to like their less advanced allies, some even forgiving the Narei and Sildaen treachery, most lie upon the dreams of their once great empire and will do anything, including sacrificing allies, simply so that they may live on and be one of the chosen upon the Eternal King's return.

SANGUINNAR RELIGIONS

The Sanguinnar's belief is centered around the return of the Eternal King. Their newly arisen God had, at the height of his power ages ago, paved the way for their glorious empire upon golden lands, removing the lesser human and orcish kind who had begun to encroach. The Eternal King gave them immortality and power in the form of crystal and left secrets in the form of blood for those who had the willpower to do so. The Sanguinnar believe it is their houses' rule to make the return of the Eternal King, even at the cost of their own kind.

SILDAEN CULTURE

The very few Sildaen live among one of the last ancient aelvenkin cities, thanking their prophet that had guided them long ago away from the Sanguinnar houses that destroyed their culture. Sildaerar, the glittering eye, is a beautiful city of crystals, silvers, gold and white marble. A Sildaen will often pray to Arenya to aid in the return of the Eternal King, thinking them to be linked in the grand scheme of things.
