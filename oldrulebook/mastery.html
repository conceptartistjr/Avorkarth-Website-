Mastery

Avorkarth is a more settled world, where the mighty and magical are rare and the skilled are just that, skilled, nothing more. Mastery represent the character's ability and practice in certain skills compared to the rest of its own species. It does not represent a general power level, but simply a character's determination and practice.

Although masteries may not give a weak character the abilities to level mountains, they still make quite an overall difference and a character with mastered skills has a much higher chance of surviving an ambush than an unleveled one.

Maximum Mastery

While the experience required to gain a level of mastery is nigh consistent for all creatures, not all creatures have the same potential of power. The highest level attainable is determined by the player, as it is their character, however if a player is unsure as to how high they may go, they may check the table below. Usually, creatures that have a longer lifespan can attain a higher level, considering they have the chance of more encounters throughout their years.

Mastery Categories

Each skill, combat bonus or any additional mastery requires a certain amount of years of practice before the bonus is achieved. The character's age, and a few talents, determine the bonus of the mastery.

The mastery categories are as follows :
- Natural Skills : These skills are those that all can do, mastery gives a few bonuses.
- Learned/Knowledge Skills : These skills are those that require training to be done well. Mastery within will give larger bonuses.
- Weapon/Combat : This mastery is based upon the years of training and combat experience the character has had. The character gains bonuses from each weapon mastery level as well, adding to the combat mastery.
